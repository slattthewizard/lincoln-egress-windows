## What Causes a Cracked Window Well Cover in Nebraska

Most window well covers are polycarbonate (clear or frosted plastic) or a metal-and-mesh bubble style. Both take a beating in Southeast Nebraska, just in different ways.

Polycarbonate covers hold up well to impact when they're new, but sunlight breaks down the resin over time. UV exposure makes the plastic go brittle and yellow, and a brittle cover cracks the next time it gets stepped on, hit by a mower, or loaded with ice. Lancaster and Gage county winters don't help. Water sits in tiny surface scratches, freezes, expands, and widens the crack with every freeze-thaw cycle from November through March.

Metal bubble covers crack less often but can bend or tear their mesh screening after a heavy snow load or a fallen branch. Either way, once the seal is broken, the well starts collecting water, leaves, and eventually mice or spiders, exactly what the cover was supposed to stop.

A few common triggers we see on service calls:
- A ladder, hose reel, or lawn equipment set down hard on the cover
- Snow shoveled or blown onto the cover and left to refreeze
- Age. Most polycarbonate covers are rated for 8-12 years before UV makes them brittle
- A cover that was never sized right for the well and flexes under weight

Once a crack forms, the damage tends to snowball. Rain and melting snow find their way through the gap and pool at the bottom of the well instead of draining away. That standing water softens the soil around the foundation, invites mosquitoes and mice, and in older wells can start working at the mortar or metal well itself. A $200 cover swap now is a lot cheaper than the well repair that follows a season or two of water sitting where it shouldn't.

## Repair or Cracked Window Well Cover Replacement? How to Decide

A hairline crack in one corner, away from foot traffic, might buy you a season with clear packing tape or a bead of exterior sealant. That's a stopgap, not a fix, and only makes sense if you already have a replacement scheduled.

Replace the cover instead of patching it if any of these are true:

- The crack runs more than a few inches or crosses the middle of the cover
- You can flex the cover with light hand pressure near the crack
- Water is pooling in the well after rain because debris is getting through
- The cover no longer sits flush or the clips/anchors are bent or missing
- It's a bedroom egress window and the cover needs to release easily from inside in an emergency

That last point matters more than people expect. Under the residential code Lincoln and Lancaster County enforce, a well cover over an emergency escape opening has to be removable from the inside without a key, tool, or special knowledge. A cracked, warped, or improperly seated cover can bind in its track and fail that test even if it still looks fine from the yard. You can see how the well and cover are supposed to fit together in the city's own [egress window and well detail diagram](https://www.lincoln.ne.gov/City/Departments/PDS/Building-Safety/Residential-Charts-and-Diagrams/Typical-Egress-Window-and-Well-Detail).

If the well itself is also rusted, bowed, or pulling away from the foundation, a new cover alone won't solve it. That's a bigger job, and one worth having looked at separately before you spend money on a cover that will just crack again against a failing well.

## Why a Cracked Cover Is a Safety and Code Problem, Not Just an Eyesore

It's easy to treat a cracked cover as cosmetic, something you'll get to eventually. Two things push it up the priority list.

First, safety. A brittle cover can give way under a child's weight, a pet, or even a delivery driver cutting across the yard, especially over a well several feet deep. A fall of even three or four feet onto concrete or gravel can cause a real injury, and it's the kind of thing homeowner's insurance adjusters ask about after a claim.

Second, code and resale value. A legal basement bedroom needs a compliant, functioning egress window and well. Appraisers and home inspectors check the well cover along with the window itself, and a damaged cover is a common flag on inspection reports. Third, pests. A gap around a cracked cover is an easy entry point for mice, voles, and insects looking for a way toward your foundation. We've pulled more than one cover in Lincoln and found a rodent nest had already moved in underneath.

## DIY Fixes vs. When to Call a Professional

Swapping a standard-size cover that sits on top of an existing, undamaged well is a job a lot of homeowners can handle with basic tools, as long as you order a cover that actually matches your well's dimensions and shape. Measure twice; a cover that's even an inch off won't seat or lock properly.

Call a professional instead if:

- You're not sure whether the well itself (not just the cover) is damaged underneath
- The window frame or glass shows separate cracking or fogging
- Any concrete cutting, foundation work, or well resizing is involved
- There's electrical conduit, an outdoor outlet, or wiring running near the well
- You need the work documented for a home sale, appraisal, or insurance claim

Concrete cutting and structural work around a foundation opening aren't DIY territory. Get those, and anything electrical near a window well, handled by a licensed pro. A wet window well next to live wiring is a real hazard, not a hypothetical one.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can I just tape or glue a cracked window well cover instead of replacing it?

Tape or exterior sealant can buy a little time on a small, non-load-bearing crack, but it won't restore the cover's strength or its watertight seal. Treat it as a temporary patch and plan on a proper cracked window well cover replacement within a season, especially before winter.

### How long does a window well cover last in Nebraska's climate?

Polycarbonate covers typically last 8 to 12 years before UV exposure and freeze-thaw cycles make them brittle enough to crack. Metal bubble and custom heavy-duty covers usually run 15 to 20 years or more, since they're less affected by sun exposure.

### Does a cracked cover affect whether my basement bedroom counts as legal?

It can. Inspectors and appraisers check that the egress cover releases easily from inside without a key or tool, and a cracked or warped cover can bind or fail that check even if the window itself is fine. Getting it fixed before a sale or inspection avoids a flagged report.

### Do I need a permit to replace a window well cover in Lincoln?

A straightforward cover swap on an existing well usually doesn't need a separate permit, but any work that changes the well's size or the window opening does. Lincoln-Lancaster County permit fees for related egress work generally run $75-$200; you can check current requirements on the city's [permit applications page](https://www.lincoln.ne.gov/City/Departments/PDS/Building-Safety/Permit-Applications).
