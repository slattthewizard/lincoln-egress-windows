## The Short Answer: Egress Window Installation Timelines by Project Type

There are really two different jobs hiding under one phrase, and they take very different amounts of time.

**Replacement in an existing opening.** If your home already has an egress-sized window and well, and you're just swapping in a new unit, most crews finish in a single day. Sometimes it's a half day if the frame is in good shape and no rot repair is needed.

**New cut-in installation.** This is what most basement bedroom projects need. A crew has to cut through the foundation, dig out and install a window well, frame the new opening, set the window, and finish the interior. Expect two to four days of physical labor, plus time for concrete to cure and drainage gravel to settle before the well cover and finish work go on.

A basement window swap that doesn't involve enlarging the opening (dropping a smaller basement window in for a bigger one) sits in between, usually one to two days depending on whether the well needs resizing too.

So when someone asks how long does egress window installation take, the real question underneath it is usually "how long until I can list my house" or "how long until the tenant can move in." That answer includes the permit and inspection window, not just the labor. We'll get to that.

## Day-by-Day: What a New Cut-In Actually Looks Like

Here's what a typical new cut-in installation looks like on the ground in Lincoln and the surrounding counties, assuming decent weather and no surprises behind the foundation wall.

**Day 1: Layout, excavation, and the cut.** The crew marks the opening, digs out the area for the window well, and cuts the new opening into the concrete or block foundation. This is the loudest, dustiest day, and it's also the one where surprises show up, like unexpected rebar placement or a foundation that's thicker than the plans suggested.

**Day 2: Framing and window set.** With the opening cut clean, the crew frames it, sets the egress window unit, and seals around the frame. If the window well needs a concrete or steel base, that goes in now too.

**Day 3: Window well and drainage.** The well itself gets installed and backfilled with gravel for drainage. This is where a lot of shortcuts happen on cheap jobs. Skipping proper drainage rock is the single biggest cause of a flooded window well two winters later.

**Day 4 (if needed): Interior finish and cleanup.** Trim, interior sill work, and any drywall patching around the new opening. On smaller projects this folds into day 3.

After the physical work, plan on the concrete curing for at least a few days before heavy backfill compaction, and if your project needs a final building inspection, that adds however long the city's inspection queue is running, often three to seven business days in Lincoln during busy season.

A family in Crete finished their cut-in in three working days last spring, but didn't get their final sign-off from the city for another nine days because of scheduling on the inspector's side, not the crew's. Build that gap into your plan if you're working toward a hard deadline like a home sale.

## What Actually Slows Down an Egress Window Installation Timeline

Four things push a project from "fast" to "why is this taking so long," and none of them are really about the window itself.

**Foundation material and thickness.** Poured concrete cuts differently than block, and older Lincoln-area foundations sometimes have thicker walls than expected. A thicker or harder wall adds hours to the cut, not days, but it can bump a tight one-day replacement into a day and a half.

**Soil and drainage conditions.** Southeast Nebraska clay soil doesn't drain quickly. If your yard already holds water after a storm, plan on extra excavation and possibly a sump connection for the well drain, which adds a day.

**Permits and inspections.** Lincoln and Lancaster County require a permit for egress window installation that involves cutting a new opening, and most jurisdictions want a final inspection before you close up the wall. Permit turnaround itself is often just a few business days, but scheduling the inspector's visit around your crew's schedule is where real time gets lost. Read our guide on [Lincoln-Lancaster County egress permit requirements](/blog/lincoln-lancaster-county-egress-permit/) before you set a hard date with anyone.

**Weather.** Frozen ground in December and January slows excavation and concrete curing significantly. If you're planning a winter project, budget extra days and read up on what changes in cold weather.

His total project cost landed around $6,200 for the full install with well and finishing, right in the normal range, but his timeline stretched from an expected week to closer to three.

## How to Keep Your Egress Window Installation on Schedule

A few practical steps keep your project moving instead of stalling out.

**Get the permit application in early.** Don't wait until the crew is scheduled. Lincoln-Lancaster County permit review can move fast when the application is complete the first time.

**Ask about soil and drainage before you sign a contract.** A contractor who's worked in your neighborhood will know if the clay content or water table around your foundation typically adds a day.

**Avoid scheduling around a hard deadline with zero buffer.** If you have a closing date or move-in date, build in at least a week of slack for inspection scheduling. The installation itself is rarely the bottleneck.

**Don't chase the fastest possible timeline at the expense of drainage work.** Skipping proper gravel backfill or a well drain connection to save a day is how you end up dealing with a flooded window well after your first spring storm instead of before.

**Know when to stop and call a professional.** Cutting a foundation opening, running new drainage tied into your home's grading, and any electrical work near a window well (lighting, sump pumps) are not DIY-safe shortcuts. A miscut foundation opening can compromise structural framing above it, and that's a repair bill nobody wants. If a job involves concrete cutting or structural work near load-bearing walls, that's a licensed crew's job, not a weekend project.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How long does egress window installation take from start to finish?

A replacement in an existing opening usually takes one day. A new cut-in with a window well typically takes two to four days of hands-on labor, but the full project, including permits and inspection scheduling, often spans one to three weeks in the Lincoln area.

### Can egress window installation be done in one day?

Yes, if you're replacing a window in an opening that's already egress-compliant and the well doesn't need work. New cut-in installations almost never finish in a single day once you count the foundation cut, framing, window set, and well backfill.

### Does winter weather slow down egress window installation in Nebraska?

Yes. Frozen ground makes excavation harder and slows concrete curing, which can add several days to a week onto a project compared to a spring or summer install. It's still doable, just plan extra buffer time and read our tips on [winter egress window installation](/blog/winter-egress-window-installation/) before scheduling.

### How long does the permit process add to my egress window timeline?

In Lincoln and Lancaster County, permit review often takes just a few business days if your application is complete, but scheduling the final inspection around your contractor's calendar can add another week. Ask your contractor to build inspection scheduling into your quoted timeline up front.
